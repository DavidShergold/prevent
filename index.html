Understanding Prevent

Recognizing signs of radicalization and taking action to safeguard individuals and communities.

What is Radicalization?

Radicalization is the process by which individuals develop extreme beliefs that may lead to harmful or dangerous actions. It can happen gradually, often influenced by exposure to extremist content, social pressures, or personal experiences. Recognizing the signs early—such as withdrawal from family, hostility toward different views, or engagement with radical groups—can help protect vulnerable individuals. By understanding radicalization and knowing how to report concerns, we can work together to safeguard communities and promote awareness.
